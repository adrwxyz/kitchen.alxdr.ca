## Ingredients

### Vegetables

- 4–5 zucchini
- 8 small potatoes
- 2 tomatoes
- 2 peppers

### Filling

- 1 cup short grain rice, soaked then strained
- 1 lb ground beef
- 1 tomato, diced

### Filling Seasoning

- 7-spice
- Sumac
- Mild Aleppo pepper flakes
- Dash no-salt seasoning mix
- Salt
- Pepper

### Cooking Liquid

- 3 tomatoes, diced and sautéed
- Bay leaf
- Coriander
- Salt
- Pepper
- Beef stock or broth (enough to cover half the pot)

## Steps

1. **Hollow the vegetables** — core the zucchini and potatoes, leaving a thin wall. Cut the tops off the tomatoes and peppers and scoop out the insides. Reserve the tops.
2. **Make the filling** — combine strained rice, ground beef, diced tomato, and all filling seasonings. Mix well.
3. **Stuff** — fill each vegetable about ¾ full (the rice will expand). Replace the tops on tomatoes and peppers.
4. **Prepare the cooking liquid** — sauté the diced tomatoes in a large pot. Add bay leaf, coriander, salt, and pepper. Pour in enough beef stock to come halfway up the pot.
5. **Arrange** — nestle the stuffed vegetables snugly in the pot in a single layer.
6. **Simmer** — bring to a gentle simmer, cover with the lid slightly ajar, and cook for ~30 minutes. Baste any vegetables sitting above the liquid line occasionally.
7. **Reduce** — remove the lid and cook uncovered for a final ~15 minutes, reducing the liquid to your liking.

Serve with warm bread and plain yoghurt.

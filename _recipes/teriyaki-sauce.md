ingredients:
  - "1/3 cup soy sauce"
  - "1/3 cup mirin (Japanese sweet cooking rice wine)"
  - "1/3 cup sake (Japanese rice wine)"
  - "3 tablespoons sugar"
  - "2-3 cloves garlic, minced (optional)"
  - "1-inch piece ginger, minced (optional)"
  - "1 tablespoon cornstarch"
  - "2 tablespoons water"
instructions:
  - "In a saucepan, add soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar. If using garlic and ginger, add them in now."
  - "Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat, stirring until the sugar completely dissolves."
  - "Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes until it starts to reduce slightly and the flavors meld."
  - "For a glaze-style thick sauce, mix the cornstarch and water in a small bowl to make a slurry. Slowly whisk the slurry into the simmering sauce, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy."
  - "Remove from heat. Let cool, then transfer to a clean jar or airtight container. Store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks."
---

This homemade teriyaki sauce is easy to customize and perfectly versatile for your favorite Asian-inspired dishes. Use as a glaze for tofu, tempeh, vegetables, fried rice, or sushi bowls.

Traditional Japanese teriyaki only uses soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar. Garlic and ginger are optional additions for extra flavor.

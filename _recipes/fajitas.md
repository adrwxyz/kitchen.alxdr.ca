ingredients:
  - section: Protein & Vegetables
    items:
      - 1 lb chicken breast, beef flank steak, or shrimp
      - 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
      - 1 large onion, sliced into strips
      - 2 bell peppers (any colour), sliced into strips
  - section: Seasoning Option A — Downshiftology (herb-forward)
    items:
      - ½ tablespoon chili powder
      - ½ tablespoon ground cumin
      - 1 teaspoon garlic powder
      - ½ teaspoon paprika
      - ½ teaspoon dried oregano
      - ½ teaspoon kosher salt
      - ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  - section: Seasoning Option B — Allrecipes (sauce-thickening)
    items:
      - 1 tablespoon cornstarch
      - 2 teaspoons chili powder
      - 1 teaspoon salt or to taste
      - 1 teaspoon ground paprika
      - 1 teaspoon white sugar
      - ½ teaspoon onion powder
      - ½ teaspoon garlic powder
      - ½ teaspoon ground cumin
      - ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  - section: To Serve
    items:
      - Flour or corn tortillas, warmed
      - Sour cream
      - Guacamole
      - Salsa
      - Shredded cheese
      - Fresh cilantro
      - Lime wedges

instructions:
  - Choose one seasoning blend (Option A or B) and combine all its ingredients in a small bowl. Mix well.
  - Slice the protein into thin strips. Toss with the seasoning blend until evenly coated. Let marinate 10–15 minutes if time allows.
  - Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet or cast-iron pan over medium-high heat until shimmering.
  - Cook the seasoned protein for 5–7 minutes, turning occasionally, until cooked through and lightly charred. Remove to a plate and tent with foil.
  - Add the remaining 1 tablespoon oil to the same pan. Sauté the sliced onion and bell peppers for 5–6 minutes until tender-crisp with some charred edges.
  - Return the protein to the pan and toss everything together for 1 minute to heat through and combine flavours.
  - Serve immediately with warm tortillas and your choice of toppings.

notes:
  - Option A (Downshiftology) is brighter and more herbaceous — great for chicken. Option B (Allrecipes) has cornstarch for a thicker sauce coating and cayenne for extra heat — ideal for steak.
  - For extra flavour, squeeze fresh lime juice over the cooked fajitas right before serving.
  - A hot cast-iron skillet gives the best char. Don't overcrowd the pan — cook in batches if needed.
